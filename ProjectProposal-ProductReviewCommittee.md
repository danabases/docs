**What is your idea or feedback?**

Establish a formal Enterprise Technology Governance Committee, a standing cross-functional review board with direct representation from each enterprise technology group. The committee would be responsible for evaluating new technology purchases, reviewing proposed enterprise solutions, and ensuring all affected teams have a seat at the table before adoption decisions are finalized. Each team would designate their own representative, ensuring groups speak for themselves rather than being represented by proxy. The committee would establish a standardized review process that includes documented feedback, transparent decision rationale communicated to all participants, and a requirement that alternative solutions be evaluated before a product is selected. Access and visibility into adopted platforms would be a baseline expectation for all relevant teams, not an afterthought.


**What problem or need does this address?**

Currently, enterprise technology products are being purchased and deployed without consistent input from all affected teams. In some cases, teams are excluded from early POCs or architecture decisions entirely. In others, feedback is collected but not acted upon or explained. Teams are sometimes represented by other groups rather than having direct involvement, meaning their requirements and concerns may not be accurately reflected in the decision. This creates downstream problems: products rolled out incorrectly, platforms that create more issues than they solve, and enterprise tools that could benefit multiple teams but are siloed to one. A governance structure would reduce these risks, improve adoption outcomes, protect technology investment, and ensure decisions reflect the full organizational picture rather than a single team's preference or timeline.


**Who would benefit?**

The entire organization. Enterprise technology teams gain direct representation and visibility into decisions that affect their environments. Leadership gains a structured, auditable process for technology adoption that reduces deployment risk and redundant spend. Project teams benefit from better-vetted solutions with broader organizational buy-in. Ultimately, the business benefits from technology investments that are evaluated thoroughly, implemented correctly, and adopted with input from the people closest to the work.
